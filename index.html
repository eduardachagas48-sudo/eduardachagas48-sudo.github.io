Selected Recent Work

Presented at ICML 2026

Current research addresses causally-valid synthetic data generation for low-resource clinical AI, and deterministic-first NLP pipelines for clinical text in Brazilian Portuguese.

Workshop on Foundation Models for Structured Data · ICML 2026

DataSynK: Causal-Symbolic EHR Synthesis for Tabular Foundation Models in Low-Resource Settings

A causal-symbolic approach to synthesizing electronic health records that preserves clinically meaningful causal structure, enabling tabular foundation model training where real EHR data is scarce or restricted.

Read the paper →

LatinX in AI (LXAI) Workshop · ICML 2026

Beyond Statistical Fidelity: Causal-Valid Synthetic EHR Generation for Low-Resource Clinical AI

Argues that statistical fidelity metrics alone are insufficient for clinical synthetic data, and proposes causal validity as the criterion that matters for downstream low-resource clinical AI.

Read the paper →

LatinX in AI (LXAI) Workshop · ICML 2026

Deterministic-First Clinical Concept Normalization in Brazilian Portuguese

A deterministic-first pipeline for normalizing clinical concepts in Brazilian Portuguese text, favoring rule-based precision over opaque neural normalization wherever it is available.
